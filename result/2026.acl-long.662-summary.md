# Comprehensive Summary of Financial Symbolic Reasoning Evaluation

This research introduces a rigorous framework for evaluating advanced capabilities in symbolic financial reasoning across Large Language Models (LLMs), moving beyond simple final answer checking to assess the entire intermediate reasoning chain.

### Core Research Area
The core area is the development and application of specialized benchmarks to evaluate **Chain-of-Thought (CoT) symbolic financial reasoning**. The work addresses the limitation of existing benchmarks by providing systematic, machine-verifiable evaluation of intermediate steps in complex financial problem-solving. FINCHAIN establishes a comprehensive resource spanning 12 major financial domains and 58 topics, structured across controlled difficulty levels: Basic, Intermediate, and Advanced.

### Architectural Configurations or Methodology
The methodology is highly systematic, involving both benchmark construction and novel evaluation metrics:

**Benchmark Construction (FINCHAIN):**
*   **Data Generation:** FINCHAIN instantiates its 58 topics using **parameterized symbolic templates**. These templates are implemented as executable Python programs that automatically generate fully verifiable reasoning steps and final answers.
*   **Rigor & Verification:** The process includes mandatory data preprocessing to resolve cross-national inconsistencies (defaulting to U.S. settings), standardize units, and restrict multi-target requests to single goals. Quality assurance is maintained through expert annotation utilizing a structured taxonomy of flaws: *Formula Choice Error*, *Math/Logic Error*, *Representation Error*, *Robustness Error*, and *Clarity Issue*.

**Evaluation Methodology (CHAIN EVAL):**
*   The proposed metric, **CHAIN EVAL**, jointly assesses final-answer correctness and step-level reasoning consistency/faithfulness. It is built upon the Normalized DTW Alignment Score ($\text{DTW}_{\text{Gate}}$), which captures global structural reasoning consistency by finding an optimal monotonic path between predicted and reference step sequences.
*   This score combines two components: **Semantic Similarity (SS)** (via cosine similarity of encoded steps) and **Answer Match (AM)** (verifying numerical/symbolic consistency at intermediate results, allowing up to a 5% tolerance).
*   The final CHAIN EVAL score is a weighted combination of the structural alignment ($\text{DTW}_{\text{Gate}}$) and Final Answer Correctness ($\text{FAC}$): $\text{CHAIN\_EVAL} = (1-\alpha) \cdot \text{DTW}_{\text{Gate}} + \alpha \cdot \text{FAC}$.
*   The evaluation also employs a detailed **error taxonomy** to categorize failures diagnostically: No Error, Computational errors, Conceptual errors, Sign/Direction errors, Unit/Precision mismatches, Parsing/Format issues, and Hallucination.

### Key Findings
1.  **Performance Gradient:** While frontier proprietary models (e.g., GPT-5-mini) generally perform best overall on symbolic reasoning under CHAIN EVAL, even they struggle significantly with advanced, multi-step tasks, showing pronounced degradation across all tested models.
2.  **Step Fidelity vs. Final Answer:** A critical finding is that achieving a correct final answer ($\text{FAC}$) does not guarantee faithful intermediate reasoning steps; the gap between $\text{CHAIN\_EVAL}$ scores and simple $\text{FAC}$ highlights this discrepancy.
3.  **Model Behavior (Diagnostic):** Stronger models tend to fail due to Computational or Conceptual mistakes, whereas weaker/lower-performing models exhibit a significantly broader and more diverse error profile, frequently characterized by substantial Parsing/Format issues or hallucinated reasoning steps.
4.  **Targeted Improvement:** Targeted fine-tuning on specialized datasets is crucial for improving multi-step reasoning fidelity; while math corpora training helps numerical generalization, narrowly focused finance tuning shows variable alignment improvements.

### Limitations and Future Work
The current framework acknowledges several constraints:
*   **Synthetic Nature:** FINCHAIN's data is entirely synthetic, lacking the linguistic diversity found in real-world financial texts.
*   **Scope Limitation:** The focus is solely on symbolic numerical reasoning, excluding qualitative tasks (e.g., risk assessment).
*   **Domain/Language Restriction:** The current system is limited to English and U.S.-centric financial conventions.
